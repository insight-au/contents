Pythagorus Theorem

One major development in geometry is the famous Pythagorus theorem, which states that the square of the hypotenuse in a right triangle is the sum of the squares of the other two sides. It is believed that Indian mathematicians provided a proof for this theorem, before Pythagorus himself did ! Let us look at some of the proofs that Indian mathematicians offered.

Bhaskara's proof for the Pythagorus theorem:

  1. ABC is a right angled triangle.
  2. Draw A'M1 || BC and B'M2 perpendicular to BC. We now have four congruent triangles , namely, A'AM3 ,BB'M2 ,B'A'M1 & ABC.
  3. Let area of triangle ABC = D.
  4. Then sum of the areas of the four triangles = 4D.
  5. Now , 4D = 4 ( * a * b) . Thus, 4D = 2ab.
    6. (where a is the length of the side opposite angle A & b is the length of the side opposite angle B.)
  6. Since AA'M3 & ABC are congruent , we have A'M3=AC=b and since B'A'M1 & ABC are congruent we have A'M1=BC=a.
  7. Therefore, we have M1M3=A'M1-A'M3 = a - b.
  8. Since area(ABB'A')=area(CM2M1M3) + 4D, we obtain c2 = (a-b)2 + 2ab. Hence, c2 = a2 + b2.

The above is a proof offered by Bhaskara for the Pythagorus theorem. Very interesting , isn't it ?

Converse of the Pythagorus theorem

Statement of the theorem :
If in a triangle ABC the sides BC=a, CA=b, AB=c satisfy the relation
    c2 = a2 + b2 ---- (1),
then the angle ACB = 90.

Proof :
Construct a Right triangle A'B'M' such that M'B' = a and M'A' = b and angle M' = 90. Let A'B' = c'. Then by Phythagoras theorem we have , c' 2 = a2 + b2. Therefore , from (1) we have c' 2 = c2 i.e. c'= c. Since c'= c, triangle ABC and A'B'M' are congruent. Consequently, angle C = angle M' = 90.
