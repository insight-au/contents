What is the solution for the equation X2 - 17Y2 = 1? This is an indeterminate equation. A trivial solution for this equation is X=1,Y=0.In fact, there exists infinite number of such integral solutions for this equation.Finding such solutions had always been a challenge for the modern mathematicians. The Indian Mathematicians of the yore have given algorithms to solve this equation .

Consider the equation,

X2 - DY2 = k where D and k are constants.
The above equation was called as Vargaprakrti by our ancient Mathematicians. Brahmagupta(born 628A.D) showed that if a Vargaprakrti with k = 1(or-1,2,-2,4,-4) had one integral solution ,then it had infinite number of integral solutions.

In his book Brahmasphuta Siddhanta, he has given the Bhavana principle as follows :

If the two Vargaprakritis

(1) X12 - DY12 = k1 and

(2) X22 - DY22 = k2, had a solution each,

then there exists a solution for the Vargaprakrti

(3) X32 - DY32 = k1k2.

The Bhavana was proved by multiplying equations (1) and (2).

X3= (X1X2+DY1Y2 , X1Y2+X2Y2) and

X3= (X1X2-DY1Y2 , X1Y2-X2Y2) are two solutions of (3).

The solution (X1X2+DY1Y2 , X1Y2+X2Y2) is called Samara Bhavana and the solution (X1X2-DY1Y2 , X1Y2-X2Y2) is called Visesha Bhavana. As a special case, if k1=k2=1, (1), (2)and (3) represent the same equation, X2 - DY2 = 1. Now,the Samara Bhavana is (X2+DY2, 2XY) and the Visesha Bhavana is (X2 - DY2,0).

For example,consider the Vargaprakrti X2 - 8Y2 = 1.Given (3,1) is a solution for this equation ,we get the Samara Bhavana as (32 + 8 x 12, 2 x 3 x 1) i.e (17,6).This is yet another solution to the given equation.The Visesha Bhavana is (32 - 8 x 12, 0) i.e (1,0).This is also a solution but is a trivial solution. Hence, given one non-trivial solution for the above equation,we can get yet another non-trivial solution. How interesting!

There is something more in store for you! Indian Mathematicians have developed a technique called Chakravala to generate as many solutions as possible from a given solution.That is, if we know one solution (which may be trivial also!) for X2 - DY2 = 1, then we can generate infinite number of solutions.
