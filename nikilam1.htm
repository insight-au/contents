A careful observation of the problem, 52 ÷ 9 would reveal the following:-

A) The quotient is 5, same as the tens digit of the dividend.
B) The remainder is 7, the sum of the tens digit and units digit.
Does this hold true for all 2-digit numbers divided by 9 ?
Consider 34 ÷ 9.

The quotient is 3 itself
The remainder is 3+4=7.

A host of other examples affirm that this technique indeed works.


A Slight Refinement

Now Consider 49 ÷ 9.

Quotient = 4
Remainder = (4 + 9) = 13 !

Which is absurd, as the remainder can never exceed the divisor. This can be set right in the following fashion. Divide 13 again by 9 ( Using the same technique )

Quotient= 1
Remainder=3

Add this Quotient with 4 to get 5 as the answer. The Remainder is 3.

The above technique can be summarised as follows:

For a Two digit number to be divided by 9, copy the tens digit as the quotient (Q) and the sum of the tens and units digit as the remainder(R).
If this R > 9, Again carry out the division process to obtain a quotient S and a remainder T. The result is Quotient=Q+S and remainder is T.

A Few Examples:

9 ) 6 1
6 (Copy the tens digit to the units digit)
6 7 (Add Tens digit to Units digit to obtain 7)

(Q=6, R=7)
