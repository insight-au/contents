Area Of Cyclic Quadrilateral

Consider the cyclic quadrilateral A1A2A3A4. Draw A1M and A3N perpendicular to the diagonal A2A4.
Let p1=A1M and p2 = A3N.
d13 is the diagonal joining the vertices A1A3
d24 is the diagonal joining the vertices A2A4

Then,
area(A1A2A4) = 1/2*p1*d24 and
area(A3A2A4) = 1/2*p2*d24

Therefore, area(A1A2A3A4) = 1/2*(p1+p2)*d24. ------(1)
Since (a4)2 = (p1)2+(A2M)2 and

(a3)2 = (p1)2+(A4M)2 ,
we have
(a4)2 - (a3)2 = (A2M)2- (A4M)2 =(A2M+A4M)(A2M-A4M) = d24.(A2M-A4M) ----(2)

Similarly , (a1)2 =(p2)2+(A2N)2 and
(a2)2 =(p2)2+(A4N)2, so that
(a2)2 - (a1)2 = (A4N)2- (A2N)2 =(A4N+A2N)(A4N-A2N) = d24.(A4N-A2N) ----(3)

From (2) and (3) , (a4)2 - (a3)2 + (a2)2 - (a1)2 =d24{(A2M-A4M) + (A4N-A2N))} =d24{(A2M-A2N)+(A4N-A4M)} =d24.2MN
Thus, MN = ((-a1)2 + (a2)2 - (a3)2 + (a4)2) / (2.d24) ------(4)

It can be seen that ( refer above figure) (d13)2 = (MN)2 + (p1+p2)2 ------------(5)
From (1), (4), (5) we have, (area(A1A2A3A4))2 = 1/4(d24)2((d13)2-(MN)2) = 1/4[ (d13)2.(d24)2 - ((-a1)2 + (a2)2 - (a3)2 + (a4)2)2 / 4 ]

The diagonals of a cyclic quadrilateral can be expressed in terms of its sides:
(d13)2 = (a1a2+a3a4)(a1a3+a2a4) / (a1a4+a2a3) -------(I)
(d24)2 = (a1a3+a2a4)(a1a4+a2a3) /(a1a2+a3a4) -------(II)
Multiplying & dividing by 4,
(area(A1A2A3A4))2 =1/16[4(a1a3+a2a4)2-((-a1)2+(a2)2-(a3)2+(a4)2)2/4](from I&II)
= 1/16 {2(a1a3+a2a4)+((-a1)2+(a2)2-(a3)2+(a4)2)}. {2(a1a3+a2a4)-((-a1)2+(a2)2-(a3)2+(a4)2)}]
=1/16[{(a1+a3)2-(a2-a4)2}.{(a2+a4)2-(a1-a3)2}]
=1/16[(a1+a3+a2-a4)(a1+a3-a2+a4) (a2+a4+a1-a3)(a2+a4-a1+a3)]
Put 2Q = a1+a2+a3+a4
(area(A1A2A3A4))2 =1/16(2.Q - 2.a4)(2.Q - 2.a2)(2.Q - 2.a3)(2.Q - 2.a1)
(area(A1A2A3A4))2=(Q - a1)(Q - a2)(Q - a3)(Q - a4)
Hence, area=sqrt((Q-a1)(Q-a2)(Q-a3)(Q-a4)).
